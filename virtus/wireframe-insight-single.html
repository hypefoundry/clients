Finance salary benchmarks: what has shifted in 2026

By Virtus Finance Team · 5 min read · March 2026

Article Hero Image

The Australian mid-market finance hiring landscape has shifted meaningfully over the past twelve months. While headline salary growth has moderated from the post-COVID peaks of 2023–24, the picture across cities and seniority levels tells a more nuanced story. This report draws on Virtus placement data and market benchmarking across Sydney, Melbourne, and Brisbane.

Sydney: steady growth across mid-market

Sydney remains the highest-paying market for mid-market finance roles, though the gap with Melbourne has narrowed. Financial Controller salaries have seen the strongest upward movement, driven by demand from scaling professional services firms and ASX-listed mid-caps looking to strengthen reporting functions.

The candidates who are moving are not chasing salary alone. They are looking for scope, autonomy, and a clear path forward. The roles that sell themselves are the ones that can articulate all three.

Melbourne: contract rates climbing

Melbourne has seen a notable increase in contract and interim demand, particularly for Senior Accountants and Financial Analysts. Day rates for experienced contractors have increased 8–12% year-on-year, reflecting both talent scarcity and employer caution around permanent headcount commitments.

Role 2025 2026
Financial Controller $150k–$180k $160k–$195k
Senior Accountant $95k–$115k $100k–$125k
Financial Analyst $90k–$110k $95k–$120k
Management Accountant $100k–$120k $105k–$130k

What this means for candidates

If you are a mid-to-senior finance professional considering a move, the market is in your favour — but only if you can clearly articulate what you bring beyond technical competence. Employers are paying premium for candidates who demonstrate commercial acumen, stakeholder management, and the ability to build and lead teams. The days of salary alone driving a move are over.

What this means for employers

The cost of a bad hire in finance is well documented — 2.5x the annual salary when you account for recruitment costs, onboarding, lost productivity, and the ripple effect on team morale. In a market where the best candidates are not actively looking, speed and decisiveness matter. The employers who are winning talent are the ones who can move from first interview to offer in under two weeks.
