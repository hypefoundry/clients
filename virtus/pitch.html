Brand Positioning © 2026

The Context

Fifteen years placing professionals through Momentum Consulting. 92% still in position at the twelve-month mark. Virtus is that track record, given a brand built for the room it operates in.

Momentum built its name in blue-collar recruitment. White-collar candidates and hiring managers need to see themselves in the brand they work with. Virtus gives them that.

Same people. Same track record. Different room.

Brand Positioning © 2026

Australian white-collar recruitment is, in brand terms, a vacuum. Virtus’s opportunity is to be the name that fills it.

The Market

Hays, Robert Half, Hudson, Randstad, PageGroup. Blue. Sans-serif. Corporate. Interchangeable. The entire sector looks like it was designed by the same person on the same afternoon.

The Opening

people2people is the exception. Genuine brand work, and it shows. But their play is breadth: seven sectors, national presence, warmth at scale. Virtus goes deeper. Five specialist sectors. The name employers and candidates reach for first, because Virtus is right, not because it’s everywhere.

Two audiences. One expectation. The recruiter already knows the answer before the call starts.

The Candidate

White-collar professionals across finance, IT, legal, HR, marketing, and related sectors. People who are performing well in stable roles. They respond to a recruiter who knows their world, respects their time, and has something genuinely relevant to say.

The Client

Mid-market employers who want fewer, better placements. People who stay and perform. They measure success in retention, not fill rate.

Positioning

Built to Deliver.

For the professionals most recruiters struggle to reach. The ones who pick up for someone who knows their sector, respects their time, and has been right before.

Specialist depth, backed by a proven track record. The right match, made by someone who already knows both sides of the conversation.

Not a promise. A track record. Momentum is the proof. Virtus is what comes next.

Brand Direction © 2026

Two Directions

Each rooted in the positioning above. Both are credible paths forward. They differ in how Virtus signals authority: one draws on heritage and provenance, the other on a bold, modern identity system.

Established Authority Direction 1
Direction 1

Established Authority

The brand symbol is derived from a Gallic coin featuring Virtus, giving the mark genuine provenance rather than borrowed gravitas. Serif typography, warm stone tones, and a gold crest emblem. The brand feels like it’s been in the room for decades.

This is a nod to the fifteen-year history behind Virtus. The team built Momentum from the ground up. That heritage is real. Direction 1 makes it visible.

The bet: warmth and gravitas signal credibility. The brand looks established without looking corporate. Distinguished, not sterile.

Direction 2

Earned Authority

Credibility isn’t inherited. It’s demonstrated. This direction draws from modern, identity-led brands that signal confidence through a strong visual system. A bold green palette, a geometric mark built from shapes in the Momentum brand, clean sans-serif typography, and dark interfaces that feel premium and modern.

The connection to Momentum is deliberate. The mark evolves familiar forms into something new, carrying the heritage forward without repeating it.

The bet: a distinctive colour and mark system cuts through a sector full of blue-and-grey corporate wallpaper. The brand looks like where the market is heading, not where it’s been.
