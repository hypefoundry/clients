Cocktail

Smoked
Old Fashioned

The quintessential smoked cocktail. Rich bourbon, aromatic bitters, and a blanket of hickory smoke transform a timeless classic into something truly theatrical.

Prep Time 5 min
Smoke Time 1–2 min
Serves 1
Difficulty Easy

Ingredients

Cocktail
  • Bourbon whiskey 60 ml
  • Simple syrup 10 ml
  • Angostura bitters 2 dashes
  • Orange peel 1 strip
  • Luxardo cherry 1
  • Large ice cube or sphere 1
For smoking
  • Hickory wood chips 1 pinch
  • Bon Apperoo Smoker 1 Glass

Method

01

Prepare the glass

Place a large ice cube or sphere into a rocks glass. A single, large piece of ice melts slowly and keeps the drink cold without diluting it too quickly.

02

Mix the cocktail

In a mixing glass, combine the bourbon, simple syrup, and two dashes of Angostura bitters. Stir gently with a bar spoon for about 20 seconds — you want it chilled and integrated, not bruised.

03

Strain over ice

Strain the mixture over the large ice cube in your rocks glass. The liquid should settle cleanly around the ice.

04

Load the smoker

Place a pinch of hickory wood chips into the Bon Apperoo 1 Glass Smoker bowl. Position the smoker on top of the glass, creating a seal.

05

Smoke it

Light the wood chips and let the smoke fill the glass for 1–2 minutes. The smoke will infuse into the liquid and cling to the ice, building layers of aroma. Remove the smoker when the glass is opaque with smoke.

06

Garnish and serve

Express the orange peel over the glass by squeezing it skin-side down, releasing the citrus oils into the smoke. Drop the peel and a Luxardo cherry into the glass. Serve immediately while the smoke still lingers.

Pro Tip

For a richer smoke profile, try cherry or apple wood chips instead of hickory. Cherry adds a subtle sweetness that pairs beautifully with bourbon's caramel notes, while apple gives a lighter, fruitier smoke.

For an extra theatrical serve, trap the smoke under a glass cloche at the table and lift it in front of your guests.

Pairs well with

Smoked Charcuterie Board

Aged salami, prosciutto, and smoked gouda — the smoky flavours in the cocktail echo beautifully against cured meats and sharp cheeses.

Dark Chocolate

A square of 70% dark chocolate with sea salt. The bitterness and depth complement the bourbon's warmth and the smoke's earthiness.

Smoked Nuts

Candied pecans or hickory-smoked almonds make an effortless bar snack that reinforces the cocktail's smoky character.
